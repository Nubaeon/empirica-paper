% Section 5: Empirical Results - Calibration Analysis

A critical question for any self-assessment framework is \textbf{calibration accuracy}: do AI-reported epistemic vectors correspond to actual task outcomes? We analyzed calibration using Bayesian belief updating across 1,190,265 evidence observations.

\subsection{Methodology}

\subsubsection{Data Filtering}

The raw dataset contains 5,759 Bayesian belief updates across 792 sessions with 1,190,265 cumulative evidence observations. We applied two principled filters:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Default Prior Exclusion}: Assessments where the AI used uninformative default priors ($\mu_0 = 0.5$) were excluded. Default priors indicate the AI skipped genuine self-assessment or the assessment was initialized but never populated with task-specific values.

    \item \textbf{Complete Workflow Filter}: Only epistemic transactions with both PREFLIGHT and POSTFLIGHT assessments were retained. Learning delta calculation requires both endpoints; incomplete workflows cannot contribute to trajectory analysis. Sessions may contain multiple transactions, each producing an independent learning pair.
\end{enumerate}

After filtering: 1,190,265 evidence observations across 456 clean learning pairs from 234 sessions. \textbf{No pairs were excluded based on outcome}---both improving and non-improving pairs are included. The 234 sessions represent 30\% of total sessions; the remaining either used default priors or had incomplete PREFLIGHT$\rightarrow$POSTFLIGHT cycles.

\subsubsection{Two Measurement Systems}

The framework maintains two distinct measurement systems that serve different purposes:

\paragraph{Learning Delta (per-session):} For each epistemic transaction, the \textbf{learning delta} measures raw vector change within a single session:

\begin{equation}
\Delta_i = V_{i,\text{POSTFLIGHT}} - V_{i,\text{PREFLIGHT}}
\end{equation}

This is a direct comparison of the AI's self-assessed vectors before and after task execution. Positive delta indicates confidence growth; for UNCERTAINTY (inverted scale), negative delta indicates confidence growth. The learning delta answers: \emph{how much did the AI's self-assessed knowledge change during this transaction?}

\paragraph{Bayesian Calibration (cross-session):} Separately, the framework maintains Bayesian beliefs about each vector's typical behavior, updated via conjugate normal-normal inference at each POSTFLIGHT. The prior is initialized from the AI's historical belief state; the POSTFLIGHT self-assessment serves as a new observation. The posterior reflects the updated belief incorporating this latest evidence.

\begin{equation}
\mu_{\text{posterior}} = \frac{\sigma^2_{\text{obs}} \cdot \mu_{\text{prior}} + \sigma^2_{\text{prior}} \cdot V_{\text{POSTFLIGHT}}}{\sigma^2_{\text{prior}} + \sigma^2_{\text{obs}}}
\end{equation}

The Bayesian system answers a different question: \emph{what are the systematic biases in self-assessment, and how should future assessments be corrected?} As evidence accumulates across sessions, belief variance decreases, producing tighter calibration corrections (\Cref{tab:calibration-convergence}).

\paragraph{Relationship:} The learning delta feeds the Bayesian system---each POSTFLIGHT observation updates the beliefs---but they measure different things. The delta captures within-session change; the beliefs capture cross-session calibration. Table~\ref{tab:learning-analysis} reports Bayesian belief means (prior and posterior) aggregated across all clean learning pairs, with the delta column representing the difference between these aggregated beliefs. These values closely approximate the mean per-session learning deltas because the Bayesian priors are anchored by PREFLIGHT assessments.

\subsection{Pattern Analysis}

Two patterns emerge from the analysis:

\paragraph{Pattern 1: Learning Produces Confidence Growth}

All vectors show movement toward higher confidence during task execution. AI systems begin tasks with conservative self-assessments and converge toward more accurate (higher) assessments as they accumulate evidence through work.

\paragraph{Pattern 2: Magnitude Varies by Vector}

COMPLETION shows the largest delta (+0.601), suggesting AI systems dramatically underestimate task progress at PREFLIGHT. ENGAGEMENT shows near-zero delta (+0.010), suggesting this vector is already well-calibrated from task onset.

\subsection{The Learning Pipeline: Why the Delta Is Real}
\label{sec:learning-pipeline}

The learning delta is not produced by a single mechanism. Seven interlocking processes contribute to measurable capability growth during task execution:

\paragraph{1. Contextual Injection at PREFLIGHT}

Before each transaction begins, semantic retrieval surfaces prior findings, dead-ends, calibration warnings, and related lessons from previous sessions. The AI does not start from zero---it starts from accumulated epistemic state. Retrieval is ranked by $\text{impact} \times \text{type\_confidence} \times \text{recency\_decay}$, where recency follows a 24-hour half-life. This ensures critical recent knowledge surfaces first while older, less relevant context decays naturally.

\paragraph{2. The Noetic Investigation Loop}

Structured exploration produces genuine understanding: reading files, forming hypotheses, logging unknowns and findings as they are discovered. The Sentinel gate enforces this discipline---praxic tools (file editing, code writing) are capability-blocked until CHECK confirms sufficient knowledge. The NOETIC $\rightarrow$ CHECK $\rightarrow$ NOETIC loop iterates until knowledge and uncertainty thresholds are met, preventing premature action.

\paragraph{3. Cognitive Immune System}

New findings challenge existing procedural knowledge. When a finding is logged, semantically similar lessons have their confidence reduced by 0.05 per match (floor at 0.3). This prevents stale knowledge from dominating and forces continuous recalibration of what is ``known.'' The metaphor is immunological: lessons are antibodies encoding successful procedures; findings are antigens that may invalidate them. The floor ensures lessons degrade gracefully rather than disappearing entirely.

\paragraph{4. Lesson Persistence and Retrieval}

Lessons---executable procedures extracted from successful patterns---survive sessions through the COLD storage layer (git notes, YAML). Each lesson has structured steps, prerequisites, corrections, and expected epistemic deltas (the vector changes it should produce when applied). The system accumulates procedural knowledge that compounds across sessions, without modifying model weights. At each PREFLIGHT, semantic search retrieves relevant lessons, giving the AI access to procedures refined across its entire operational history.

\paragraph{5. Epistemic Intent Tracking}

Assumptions are logged with confidence scores and urgency that increases with age: $\text{urgency} = \min(1.0,\; \frac{\text{age\_days}}{30} \times (1 - \text{confidence}))$. Unverified beliefs surface with increasing priority at each PREFLIGHT, ensuring they cannot be indefinitely deferred. Decisions create permanent audit trails recording what was chosen, why, and with what reversibility. Intent edges track which noetic artifacts informed which praxic outcomes---provenance that enables post-hoc analysis of what investigation actually led to what action.

\paragraph{6. Grounded Verification}

POST-TEST objective evidence anchors self-assessment in reality. Test results, git metrics, goal completion rates, and artifact counts are collected automatically and compared against the AI's self-assessed vectors. The gap between Track~1 (self-referential delta) and Track~2 (grounded verification) reveals whether learning is genuine or self-deception. Currently 9 of 13 vectors are grounded in objective evidence (\Cref{sec:grounded-verification}).

\paragraph{7. Epistemically Weighted Compaction Recovery}

When context is compressed (a routine event in long-running sessions), recovery is ranked by epistemic weight ($\text{impact} \times \text{type\_confidence} \times \text{recency\_decay}$), not chronological order. Critical knowledge survives compaction; low-impact context is shed. This enables sessions that span compaction boundaries without losing what matters---the AI resumes with its most valuable epistemic state intact.

\medskip
\noindent Together, these seven mechanisms form a pipeline where each stage reinforces the others: contextual injection provides starting knowledge, the noetic loop builds on it, the immune system keeps it fresh, lessons accumulate it across sessions, intent tracking provides provenance, grounded verification validates it, and compaction recovery preserves it. The learning delta we measure is the compound effect of this pipeline operating on each transaction.

\subsection{Calibration Correction}

The learning delta reveals how self-assessments should be corrected. For PREFLIGHT assessments, adding the expected delta yields a more accurate estimate:

\begin{equation}
V_{\text{corrected}} = V_{\text{PREFLIGHT}} + \Delta_V
\end{equation}

For the Sentinel readiness gate (\Cref{sec:sentinel}), this means:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Raw PREFLIGHT know of 0.55 $\rightarrow$ corrected to $\sim$0.75
    \item Raw PREFLIGHT uncertainty of 0.48 $\rightarrow$ corrected to $\sim$0.27
\end{itemize}

The correction compensates for systematic conservatism at task onset.

\subsection{Grounded Verification: Dual-Track Calibration}
\label{sec:grounded-verification}

The self-referential calibration track (PREFLIGHT $\rightarrow$ POSTFLIGHT) measures learning trajectory but cannot answer a harder question: \emph{does the AI's post-task self-assessment match what actually happened?} To address this, we introduced a grounded verification track that compares POSTFLIGHT vectors against objective post-test evidence.

\subsubsection{Evidence Collection}

After each POSTFLIGHT, the POST-TEST phase automatically collects available evidence: goal/subtask completion ratios, artifact productivity (findings vs dead-ends), Sentinel gate decisions, and when available, test results and git metrics. Each evidence source has a quality weight (objective = 1.0, semi-objective = 0.7, inferred = 0.4) that modulates its influence on the grounded estimate.

Three patterns emerge:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{IMPACT shows the largest overestimation} (+0.625). This suggests impact is particularly susceptible to optimistic self-evaluation.

    \item \textbf{CHANGE and SIGNAL show strong underestimation} ($-$0.256 and $-$0.139 respectively). The AI underestimates its progress tracking and signal quality---objective evidence (git metrics, productive exploration ratios, artifact counts) indicates better performance than claimed. DO and COMPLETION show slight overestimation (+0.067 and +0.038 respectively), indicating minor optimism about execution capability.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{Track Divergence}

The self-referential track reports COMPLETION correction of +0.601 (strong underestimation at PREFLIGHT). The grounded track shows COMPLETION divergence of only +0.038 at POSTFLIGHT. This means the self-referential correction accurately captures real learning: the AI genuinely starts very pessimistic about completion, and by POSTFLIGHT its self-assessment closely matches objective evidence. The two tracks are complementary---Track 1 captures the learning trajectory, Track 2 validates (and corrects) the endpoint.

Grounded verification now covers 9 of 13 vectors (69\%) with 1,190 beliefs and 288 post-test verifications, a substantial expansion from earlier versions. This coverage increase reflects richer evidence collection across goals, artifacts, Sentinel data, git metrics, and test results.

\subsubsection{Implications}

Grounded verification shifts the epistemic landscape in three ways:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Limitation \#2 is substantially addressed}: Self-assessment is no longer purely self-referential. With 9 vectors grounded through 1,190 beliefs and 288 verifications, most vectors now have objective correlates against which calibration accuracy can be measured.

    \item \textbf{Dual-track corrections are more trustworthy}: Where grounded evidence exists, the lower observation variance ($\sigma^2 = 0.05$ vs $0.10$) gives it stronger pull on the posterior, anchoring corrections in what happened rather than what the AI said happened.

    \item \textbf{Vector-specific bias patterns emerge}: IMPACT shows large overestimation (+0.625), while CHANGE shows strong underestimation ($-$0.256) and SIGNAL shows persistent underestimation ($-$0.139). These patterns enable targeted correction: IMPACT self-assessments should be discounted, while CHANGE and SIGNAL assessments can be trusted or even boosted. KNOW's near-zero divergence (+0.008) suggests the central knowledge vector is well-calibrated after Bayesian updating.
\end{enumerate}

With 9 of 13 vectors grounded, the grounded track now provides reliable calibration corrections for most vectors. Four vectors (clarity, coherence, density, engagement) remain ungrounded due to lack of objective correlates---these may require human evaluation or downstream task metrics for external validation.

\subsection{System Evolution: A Living Calibration Loop}

The data reported here comes from a \textbf{production system that calibrates through its own operation}---not a static benchmark. This distinction matters for interpreting the results.

\subsubsection{The Declining Delta}

Early measurements showed a mean KNOW delta of +0.200. Current measurements show +0.114. This decline is not a weakness---it is \textbf{evidence the calibration loop works}. Three factors explain the shift:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Sentinel gating became more rigorous}: Early sessions often proceeded without CHECK phase enforcement. The Sentinel gate (requiring KNOW $\geq$ 0.70 and UNCERTAINTY $\leq$ 0.35 before action) was not consistently enforced. Later sessions with rigorous gating show more moderate deltas because investigation produces genuine knowledge gains before POSTFLIGHT.

    \item \textbf{Evidence collection evolved}: Early post-test verification collected limited evidence. Current implementation includes phase-aware collection (separating noetic investigation evidence from praxic action evidence), scope-weighted artifact counts (unscoped unknowns weighted at 0.3$\times$ to prevent artificial depression of KNOW grounding), and richer evidence sources (git reflog analysis, commit timing patterns, file churn metrics).

    \item \textbf{Calibration corrections compound}: Each session's learning delta feeds back into bias corrections via Bayesian updating. Over 456 learning pairs, systematic biases become well-characterized. An AI that consistently underestimated COMPLETION by +0.50 in early sessions now receives a pre-applied correction, producing more accurate PREFLIGHT assessments and thus smaller deltas. The shrinking delta reflects \emph{successful calibration}, not measurement degradation.
\end{enumerate}

\subsubsection{What the AI Never Sees}

A critical architectural property: the AI performing self-assessment \textbf{never sees its previous calibration state}. Each transaction begins fresh. The AI measures its current epistemic state via structured prompts (PREFLIGHT vectors). The Sentinel applies learned corrections when evaluating readiness (CHECK phase). Post-test evidence is collected after the AI submits POSTFLIGHT. The grounded calibration gap---comparing self-assessment to objective evidence---is computed by the framework, not the AI.

This is fundamentally different from RLHF, which provides reward signals that the model learns to optimize. Here, there is no training signal. The AI's weights remain fixed. Calibration corrections are external scaffolding stored in the framework's database, applied by the Sentinel, and invisible to the AI's self-assessment process. The AI cannot learn to game the calibration because it does not observe the calibration.

\subsubsection{Measurement Quality Over Time}

Figure~\ref{fig:calibration-convergence} shows variance dropping 107$\times$ as evidence accumulates. This convergence is the signature of a functional Bayesian system: more observations produce tighter estimates. The system becomes more confident in its calibration---not because the AI improves, but because the framework accumulates more evidence about the AI's systematic biases.

\subsection{Limitations}

Several limitations constrain interpretation:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Observational design}: Data derives from naturally-occurring sessions, not controlled experiments with external ground truth.

    \item \textbf{Self-referential measurement}: POSTFLIGHT assessments are self-reported, and the learning delta measures self-consistency rather than accuracy against external ground truth. The grounded verification track (\Cref{tab:grounded-gaps}) substantially addresses this by anchoring POSTFLIGHT assessments in objective evidence. Coverage has reached 9 of 13 vectors with 1,190 beliefs and 288 post-test verifications. However, full external validation---human evaluation, controlled test suites, downstream task performance metrics---remains an open priority for complete calibration validation.

    \item \textbf{Domain-dependent vectors}: IMPACT shows the largest grounded divergence (+0.625), but this may reflect measurement methodology rather than true miscalibration. Impact is inherently domain-specific---what constitutes ``impact'' in code (bugs fixed, test coverage) differs fundamentally from research (novel findings, methodology advances) or business contexts (revenue, adoption). The current evidence proxies (issue resolution, downstream effects) may not capture domain-appropriate impact measures. IMPACT may require domain-specific grounding rules rather than universal evidence collection.

    \item \textbf{Evolving methodology}: The measurements reported here represent a snapshot of an evolving system. As evidence has accumulated and methodology has matured, calibration has become more nuanced---early versions showed different patterns (e.g., initial DO/COMPLETION overestimation that resolved with scope-aware weighting). Historic Bayesian trajectories showing methodology evolution are available but outside the scope of this analysis. Current figures represent the most refined measurement approach.

    \item \textbf{Model concentration}: The majority of observations derive from Claude (Anthropic). Cross-model replication is needed.

    \item \textbf{Temporal confounding}: Data spans framework development. Patterns may reflect evolution rather than stable AI properties.

    \item \textbf{Selection effects}: The 456 clean learning pairs (from 234 sessions, 30\% of total) are those with complete PREFLIGHT$\rightarrow$POSTFLIGHT workflows and non-default priors. Sessions with incomplete workflows or default assessments were excluded for methodological reasons (learning delta requires both endpoints), not outcome-based selection. However, sessions that completed the full workflow may systematically differ from those that did not.

    \item \textbf{Learning trajectory vs Bayesian calibration}: The framework maintains two separate measurement systems (\Cref{sec:results}). The \emph{learning delta} measures raw vector change within a single transaction (POSTFLIGHT $-$ PREFLIGHT). The \emph{Bayesian belief system} maintains cross-session calibration by treating each POSTFLIGHT as an observation that updates a running belief state. Table~\ref{tab:learning-analysis} reports Bayesian belief means, which closely approximate mean per-session deltas but are not identical to them. The ``capability vectors'' terminology used throughout this paper refers to the 12 non-uncertainty vectors (KNOW, DO, CONTEXT, etc.), not to claims about fixed AI capabilities. These vectors measure task-specific interpretation quality---how complete and grounded the AI's understanding is for a given task---not stable underlying model capability. Model weights remain fixed; what changes is the external scaffolding's accumulated knowledge about systematic biases.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Implications}

The unified confidence growth pattern---all 13 vectors moving toward higher confidence during learning---has several interpretations:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Functional validity}: Self-assessment tracks actual epistemic state, with conservative priors corrected through evidence accumulation.

    \item \textbf{Training-induced conservatism}: RLHF and similar techniques may induce systematic underconfidence, which task execution naturally corrects.

    \item \textbf{Learning signature}: The pattern may simply reflect that doing work increases confidence---a tautology of task completion.
\end{enumerate}

The present contribution is empirical: \textbf{systematic confidence growth during learning is measurable and consistent}. The correction mechanism provides practical value regardless of causal interpretation.
